  • 2025.07 - Present

    New York, NY, USA

    Physiological and Psychological Responses to Urban Greenery

    Research Assistant · NYU Center for Urban Science + Progress; University of Florida; Fordham University

    Supervisors: Prof. Anton Rozhkov, Prof. Prince Amegbor, Prof. Zhaoxi Zhang, Prof. H. Shellae Versey

    An immersive experiment using virtual reality and multimodal measurement to study context-specific greenery and its effects on human physiology and psychology.

    • Developed a controlled framework combining immersive virtual reality with multimodal biosensing (EEG, EDA) to investigate human responses to urban greenery exposure.
    • Captured panoramic videos across New York City sites and quantified greenery levels using Green View Index, employing AI-assisted visual control to isolate greenery effects across urban contexts.
    • Launching controlled VR lab sessions (target n≈80), finalizing standardized GVI-tiered protocols and stimuli sets, and conducting initial analyses to inform health-oriented streetscape design.

    Research Assistant · NYU, People+Technology Lab
